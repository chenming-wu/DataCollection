Tutorial

With the mature of desktop 3D printers, people are gradually falling love with this rapid and convenient tools to build 3D models. One of the key problems in desktop 3D printers is the limited printing size, which impedes their wide range applications. This problem has been well studied in research fields, most of the proposed methods decompose a given large-size model into several printable sub-parts which can be assembled by means of connectors, screws, glues or self-interlocking. In the following experiments, you will be given some pairs resulted from the state-of-art algorithms, what you need to do is to select which one you favors better.
